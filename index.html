Six of Crows
Leigh Bardugo
"I just finished Six of Crows, and I can honestly say it lived up to the hype. What I loved most about this book was the characters. At first, it was a little difficult keeping track of six different points of view, but after a few chapters I became completely invested in every single one of them. Kaz, Inej, Nina, Matthias, Jesper, and Wylan all felt like real people with complicated pasts, strengths, and flaws. Instead of reading for the plot alone, I found myself reading because I genuinely cared about what happened to each character. Kaz Brekker was probably the most fascinating character for me. He's ruthless, intelligent, and always seems ten steps ahead of everyone else, but the book slowly reveals the pain and vulnerability hidden beneath his cold exterior. Inej was another standout. Her strength, resilience, and quiet determination made her incredibly easy to root for. The plot was exciting from beginning to end. The heist itself was creative and full of unexpected twists, and I never felt completely sure that the characters would succeed. Every time I thought I knew what was going to happen, the story surprised me. The pacing was especially strong in the second half, where I found it almost impossible to put the book down. One thing I appreciated was how morally gray the characters were. None of them are traditional heroes, yet they're still deeply sympathetic. The book explores themes of trust, loyalty, revenge, trauma, and survival in a way that feels mature and meaningful without becoming overly heavy. The world-building was also impressive. Although Six of Crows takes place in the Grishaverse, I never felt lost despite not knowing every detail about the world beforehand. Ketterdam felt alive, dangerous, and realistic, almost like another character in the story. If I had one criticism, it would be that the beginning was slightly confusing. The book throws readers into the world without much explanation, and it took me some time to understand the politics, locations, and magic system. However, once everything clicked, the story became much more enjoyable. Overall, Six of Crows is one of the best fantasy books I've read in a long time. It combines unforgettable characters, clever plotting, emotional depth, and high-stakes action into a story that keeps you engaged from start to finish. The ending left me desperate to pick up the sequel immediately, which is probably the highest compliment I can give a book."
